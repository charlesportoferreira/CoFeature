a week of prolonged and heavy rains in ivory coast's cocoa belt has boosted prospects for a large mid crop (may sept), but more rain is needed, up country buyers and crop analysts said on thursday.. "we have had heavy rains of five or six hours a day for the past week so that is very good," said a leading agent working in central areas around gagnoa and daloa.. crop analysts said rains favoured late mid crop pod setting and overall bean sizes after drought from mid january to late february threatened harvest volumes..
