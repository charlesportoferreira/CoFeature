a hong kong government aircraft is to be the first to land at the territory's new chek lap kok airport next month, a civil aviation department spokesman told reuters.. a beechcraft super king , belonging to the government flying service, and normally used for search and rescue missions, is due to make the maiden landing on february , the spokesman said.. initial reports said two american pilots flying a u.s. government federal aviation administration (faa) jet used to test the new airport's navigation instruments would be the first to land on the newly completed , metre long and sixty metres wide southern runway.. but for "ceremonial" reasons the spokesman said the honour will be given to hong kong government pilots watched by invited guests and the media..
